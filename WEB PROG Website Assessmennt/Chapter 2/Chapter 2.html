Fearful that her ailing father will be drafted into the Chinese military, Mulan (Ming-Na Wen) takes his spot though, as a girl living under a patriarchal regime, she is technically unqualified to serve. She cleverly impersonates a man and goes off to train with fellow recruits. Accompanied by her dragon, Mushu (Eddie Murphy), she uses her smarts to help ward off a Hun invasion, falling in love with a dashing captain along the way.

    • Classic Boxing Matches ★★★☆☆

  • "My ideal woman is courageous." Mulan is the live action retelling of the 1998 Disney animated classic. The story is relatively similar but a few people have been changed or missing altogether. The plot involves a young woman called Mulan, who is gifted with incredible chi and able to achieve physical feats most men cannot. When the Chinese army requests that all families send one male member into war against the Rourans, Mulan's crippled father answers the call. Mulan does not wish to see her elderly father risk his life though, so, she instead steals her father's sword and armor and pretends to me a man taking his place. Mulan makes many new friends and continues to develop her chi but she also has to keep her distance from others incase they find out her secret... It's a very bright and colorful film and there is rarely a slow moment, yet somehow it feels hollow. The action on screen didn't illicit much excitement and the story moves at such a brisk pace most of the time it was hard for me to build any emotional attachment to the characters. The fighting scenes were well choreographed though and there was some unique sword play but ultimately, I don't think it brought anything new to the table, I've seen similar stunts done better elsewhere. It's an innocent, family friendly action adventure and I'm sure the dazzling colors at the very least will impress many!
